# Wanderworld — 3-Tree Talent System (First Pass v1)

Source talents: Talent_Ideas_v2.txt. IDs in [brackets] map back to that file so you
can cross-reference. NEW = a node I added to balance a tree (not in the ideas file).

================================================================================
## DESIGN MODEL
================================================================================

Three trees, each with its own identity:
- ABILITIES — what your buttons do: scale Attack / Rend / Blast / Buff + crit damage.
- DEFENSE   — staying alive: mitigation, healing, boss control.
- MISC      — the engine: resource economy, energize, free-cast tempo, the opener.

"Balanced" here means **balanced in desirability**, not identical node counts:
no tree is a must-have or a trap, every point competes, and early/mid builds
should *want* to dip across trees (see CROSS-TREE SYNERGY). End-game can go wacky.

### Tier gating
Tiers unlock by points spent **in that tree** (not total):
- Tier 1: 0 spent
- Tier 2: 6 spent in tree
- Tier 3: 14 spent in tree
- Tier 4 (capstones / endcaps): 22 spent in tree

### Legend
- E   = endcap. A tree can hold several; they're optional, often "no cap" point sinks.
- #-# = requires the node listed as its prereq to be MAXED first.
- (n) = number of ranks (= points to max that node).

### Free-cast (global definition)
A free-cast means the ability costs **no resource**. The Global CD **still applies**
— a free-cast does not skip the GCD. The only thing that touches the GCD anywhere in
the trees is [F4] (Attack crits shave the GCD). Used by C5, D3, E5, E6 — all identical.

================================================================================
## POINT BUDGET (first pass — see note)
================================================================================

Non-infinite points to fully clear each tree (endcaps excluded, they overflow):

  ABILITIES  ~70
  MISC       ~71
  DEFENSE    ~61  <-- +5 from NEW [block]; still ~13% light. See OPEN QUESTIONS.

A run hands out small, frequent points, so nobody clears a tree in early/mid —
you specialize down a branch. The Defense gap is called out below rather than
papered over.

================================================================================
## TREE 1 — ABILITIES
================================================================================
Identity: raw output. Branches let you specialize (Attack / Rend / Blast / Crit /
Buff). You can't afford all branches early, which is where build identity comes from.

--- Tier 1 (0 spent) ---
[A1] (5)  +2/4/6/8/10% damage to Attack, Rend, and Blast.        <- universal opener
[C1] (3)  +crit chance 2.5% per rank (max +8% total).
[I1] (5)  +Buff damage 2/4/6/8/10%.

--- Tier 2 (6 spent) — branches open ---
ATTACK/REND BRIDGE:
[F3] (5)  Attack: 20/40/60/80/100% chance to extend Rend by 3s instead of 2s.
REND:
[G2] (5)  +Rend base time 1/2/3/4/5s.
BLAST:
[H3] (5)  Blast: 1/2/3/4/5% chance to deal an additional 50% damage.
BUFF:
[I2] (5)  +Buff duration 1/2/3/4/5s.
TEMPO:
[A3] (3)  +1/2/3% stacking damage (max 3 stacks) for keeping GCDs <1.4s apart.

--- Tier 3 (14 spent) ---
CRIT CORE:
[C2] (5)  Crits deal 2.1/2.2/2.3/2.4/2.5x.
[C6] (1)  +3% crit chance per ability point consumed on Rend & Blast.
[C3] (5)  Crits grant 1/2/3/4/5 resources.                       <- feeds the economy
REND:
[G3] (5)  +Rend max tick increases from Attack by 1-5 (11 total).
[G4] (5)  Rend ticks: 20-100% chance to add 2 resource per tick. <- feeds the economy
BLAST:
[H2] (5)  +Blast crit multiplier .2/.4/.6/.8/1x.
BUFF:
[I3] (5)  Rend/Blast: 4/8/12/16/20% per point consumed to extend Buff by 2s.
TEMPO:
[A4] (3)  Every 15/12/9 casts, next ability +20% damage.

--- Tier 4 (22 spent) — capstones ---
[F1] E    +Attack damage 2.5% (no cap).
[G1] E    +Rend tick damage 2.5% (no cap).
[H1] E    +Blast damage 2.5% (no cap).
[C4] (1)  3 Attack-or-Blast crits in a row (any combination) -> next ability's
          damage x2, applied MULTIPLICATIVELY (stacks on top of C2/H2).
[C5] E #-#(C2)  Attack crits: .5/1/1.5/2/2.5% chance to grant a free-cast.

Synergy note: Crit (C-branch) is the spine — C1->C2->C6 makes every finisher a
near-guaranteed big crit, and C3/G4 turn that into resources, so a crit build
partly funds its own economy (eases reliance on MISC). Buff (I1/I2/I3) wants you
already spamming Rend/Blast, so it pairs with the Rend or Blast branch, not alone.

================================================================================
## TREE 2 — DEFENSE
================================================================================
Identity: survival + boss control + healing. Note Blast's boss-debuffs ([H4]/[H5])
and Attack's self-heal ([F2]) live here, so an offense build that uses those
abilities is naturally pulled to dip Defense.

--- Tier 1 (0 spent) ---
[K1] E    +max HP 10% (no cap).
[J5] (5)  Passively regenerate 10/20/30/40/50 HP/sec.
[B6] (5)  Reduce damage taken 10% for first 5/10/15/20/25s of encounter.

--- Tier 2 (6 spent) ---
[K2] (3)  +2.5% dodge per rank (max +8%).
[K6] (3)  Reduce boss crits to 1.9/1.8/1.7x (from 2x).
[J3] (5)  +Heal 20/40/60/80/100% when below 30% HP.
[H4] (5)  Blast increases time to next boss attack by .2-1s.     <- offense->defense
[B5] (5)  20-100% chance to avoid first damage in first 10s.
NEW [block] (5)  2/4/6/8/10% chance to halve an incoming hit (crits included). <- anti-burst

--- Tier 3 (14 spent) ---
[K3] (3)  Each second un-hit, next hit -1/2/3% (stacks 10x).
[K5] (3)  Boss crit on you -> +100% crit chance on your next Attack/Blast.
[J1] E    +Heal amount 2.5% (no cap).
[H5] (5)  Blast reduces boss damage 10-50% for next 5s.          <- offense->defense
[F2] (5)  Attack crit at 5 points heals .5-2.5% of damage done.  <- crit->sustain

--- Tier 4 (22 spent) — capstones ---
[K7] (5)  -4/8/12/16/20% damage per point consumed for 1s after Rend/Blast cast.
[J2] E    +Heal damage 10% (no cap).
[J4] (3)  Overheal HoT 100/150/200% of overheal over 6s (once / 10s).
[K4] E #-#(K3)  A killing blow leaves you at 1 HP instead (once / encounter).

Synergy note: K7 and K5 are gated behind casting Rend/Blast and getting hit, so a
"bruiser" still has to run the offense rotation — it can't turtle passively. K7's
per-point scaling means it competes with C6/I3 for your ability points: you choose
whether a 5-point finisher buys damage, buff uptime, or a mitigation window.

================================================================================
## TREE 3 — MISC
================================================================================
Identity: the resource/tempo engine + the opener. This tree fuels the other two
(points to spend, energy to cast, free-casts to chain), which is the main reason
to mix trees rather than tunnel one.

--- Tier 1 (0 spent) ---
[E4] (3)  +Regen 3/6/10 per tick.
[B2] (5)  Start encounter with 1/2/3/4/5 ability points.
[A5] (2)  +player speed 10% (max +20%).

--- Tier 2 (6 spent) ---
[E1] (5)  All finishers cost -1/2/3/4/5.
[E3] (3)  Passive trickle: 20/40/60% chance to proc +2 resource.
[D1] (5)  Energize set-point: raises where Energize lands you, 60 -> 62/64/66/68/70
          by rank. Does NOT touch the passive tick.
[B1] (5)  +5/10/15/20/25% damage to all abilities in first 5s.
[A2] (3)  +20/25/30% damage to boss under 25% HP.

--- Tier 3 (14 spent) ---
[E2] (3)  Max resource is now 110/120/130.
[D2] (5)  Energize: 20-100% chance to add an ability point.
[D4] (3)  Energize cast while above 60% of max (checked at cast time, before the
          set) -> +3/6/10% damage for 10s. The "not a total waste" consolation.
[E5] (5)  +free-cast chance/sec by .2/.4/.6/.8/1%.
[B3] (5) #-#(B2)  Start with 1-5 points worth of Buff active.

--- Tier 4 (22 spent) — capstones ---
[D3] (3)  Energize: 3/6/10% chance to proc a free-cast.
[E6] (5)  Free-cast Rend/Blast/Buff: 4-20% per point consumed to return 10 resource.
[B4] (5) #-#(B3)  +20-60% crit chance on first ability.
[F4] E    Attack crits reduce the Global CD by .02-.1s for that cast.

Synergy note: B2 (start points) + D2 (energize -> points) are the point factory that
every point-spender in the other two trees (C6, I3, K7) runs on. E1 (cheaper
finishers) + free-casts (E5/D3/E6) let you fire those spenders more often. So MISC
doesn't deal damage itself — it multiplies whatever you built elsewhere.

================================================================================
## CROSS-TREE SYNERGY (the early/mid "want to mix" hooks)
================================================================================
- POINT ECONOMY: MISC makes ability points (B2, D2); ABILITIES (C6, I3) and DEFENSE
  (K7) spend them. Going pure-MISC has nothing to spend on; going pure-spender
  starves. Early builds want a foot in both.
- BLAST -> DEFENSE: H4/H5 sit in Defense but trigger off Blast (Abilities). A Blast
  build gets boss control "for free" by dipping Defense.
- CRIT -> ECONOMY: C3 (crits -> resources) and G4 (Rend -> resources) let a crit/Rend
  ABILITIES build lean less on MISC regen, freeing points for DEFENSE.
- CADENCE -> MITIGATION: K7 (Defense) and I3 (Buff uptime, Abilities) both reward
  casting Rend/Blast on cadence, so the same rotation feeds offense, buff, and defense.

## SAMPLE EARLY/MID BUILDS (to sanity-check the mixing)
================================================================================
1. Crit-Blast: ABIL [C1,C2,H2,H3,C6] + MISC [B2,D2] (points to fuel C6) + DEFENSE
   dip [H4] (Blast already cast). Wants all three trees by mid-game.
2. Rend-DoT:  ABIL [G2,G3,G4,I1,I3] + MISC [E4,E2] + DEFENSE [J5]. Self-funds via G4.
3. Bruiser:   DEFENSE [K1,K2,K3,K7] + MISC [B2,D2,E1] + ABIL [G2/H3] to power K7.
